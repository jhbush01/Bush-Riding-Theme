About this route

The course starts and finishes at the Seymour Racecourse, and enjoys 82% of quality gravel riding. There’s a flat, paved 2.5km neutral start before turning left onto gravel roads. At the 25km mark, the course turns left, and heads on on the first of two (2) 43km loops, before returning to Seymour. At the 30km mark, than again at the 73km mark, is the crux of the course; the Wicket Hill Climb. While only 1km in length, the section gains 125m and averages 11%.

The course is mostly rolling, enjoying wide open gravel roads interspersed with the relief of some short, paved road stretches. The elevation ranges from 155m above sea level up to 520m, when passing by the Wallaby Gully Flora Reserve. The finish is again made up of a short paved section before returning to the Racecourse and the event village.
