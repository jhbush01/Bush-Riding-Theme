About this route

The Glass House mountains. As beautiful as they are rugged. The first of many routes I will be adding to the map in this area is the Glass House Station to Woodford Loop.

Starting off in the quaint township of Glass House Mountains, you can park at the train station which is in walkable distance to the Glass House Tavern if you fancy a pint post-ride.

The first half of this loop takes you through Tibrogargan National Park, out and through Beerburrum and then out into the pine plantations where you will find the terrain you have been searching for.

Hundreds of gravel roads connect the vast pine plantations across the Sunshine Coast. You could spend every weekend trying to map them all, thankfully we have some locals who have done that hard yakka for us and we get to enjoy the fruits of their labour (Check our Glass House Gravel on Strava).

As much as they are great for us cyclists, they are also very popular in the local dirt bike and 4WD scene. So you can expect to be pleasantly surprised when a 2-stroke barks past at break neck speed, ripping you out of your silent pedal stroke rhythm. I always give them a wave and mostly wave back or nod with a acknowledgement of our shared two wheel obsession albeit slightly different engines. The small minority that stare me up and down, I just assume they’re jealous of my analogue, outback traversing abilities. In all seriousness though (definitely has nothing to do with my tight shorts).

25kms in you will face the biggest elevation gain of the day, climbing around 120m over 4kms. It’s a grind but push through, it’s now a 10km downhill into Woodford. Once you arrive you are spoilt for choices in terms of snack stops. I chose the IGA, they had a hotbox I happily raided. As I sat, ate and hydrated, I looked across and saw fellow gravel riders at CJ Pastries. Some old codgers (who would probably hate me calling them old) enjoying what was a bright blue winters day here in QLD. As they started to head off I was hoping I’d be going to same direction however the opposite was true. Good to know that there are other route options coming out of Woodford as I wouldn’t mind going back to try some of the pastries they were smashing.

Back on the road, you ride along some bitumen for a while until you turn off onto what is signed as “NO ENTRY - ROADWORKS”. As a cyclist is known to do, I ignored these signs and proceeded to venture up this road until I found reason to change my route. The road was in the middle of being graded. So think extremely bumpy dried excavator tracks and soft dirt. Luckily it hadn’t rained, if this was wet it would have been a muddy mess. But nevertheless, this road is where you climb 100m. I pray that this road is finished soon as this was the only real negative of the ride, my suspension-less gravel fork was tested that’s for sure as were my wrists.

As you come out of that climb you are back riding along plantation roads all the way until you reach the Glass House Mountains lookout. Take a moment here, breathe it in. Then go and bomb the hill + a couple more pinches before you are back safely (I hope) at your car and sipping on golden nectar from the tavern (I hope).

Vetted by Jarrod Hyslop.
